CRIME PATTERN ANALYSIS REPORT
Bengaluru Urban District — December 2025
Prepared by: State Crime Records Bureau (SCRB)
Date: 2026-01-08

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY:
December 2025 saw a 22% increase in property crimes across Bengaluru Urban District
compared to November 2025. Vehicle thefts accounted for 38% of all property crimes.
Cyber fraud cases rose sharply by 45% with most victims in the 35-55 age group.

HOTSPOT ANALYSIS:
1. Whitefield Zone (HIGH RISK):
   - 12 assault cases (up from 5 in Nov 2025)
   - Pattern: Evening hours (18:00 - 22:00), near bus stops and metro stations
   - Suspect profile: Opportunistic robbers, groups of 2

2. Koramangala Zone (MEDIUM-HIGH RISK):
   - 8 vehicle thefts, 4 cyber fraud cases
   - Vehicle theft pattern: Parking areas near restaurants/clubs, 21:00-23:00
   - Cyber fraud: KYC phishing calls, targeting IT professionals

3. HSR Layout Zone (MEDIUM RISK):
   - Drug-related arrests: 3 cases
   - Primarily cannabis and synthetic drugs
   - College/tech park proximity is a risk factor

MODUS OPERANDI PATTERNS:
- Vehicle theft gang possibly operating across multiple zones
- CCTV footage from 3 different locations shows similar vehicle descriptions
- Cyber fraud callers using spoofed SBI/HDFC numbers — potential call centre operation

RECOMMENDATIONS:
1. Increase foot patrol near Whitefield bus stops between 18:00-22:00
2. Deploy CCTV monitoring at identified vehicle theft hotspots
3. Coordinate with Cyber Crime Cell for financial tracking of fraud proceeds
4. Establish informant network near BTM Layout drug corridor

SEASONAL TREND:
December traditionally sees 15-20% increase in property crimes due to holiday gatherings,
increased cash transactions, and reduced police visibility. This year's spike (22%)
is above the historical average and warrants enhanced surveillance.
